1.1 - Introduction

 

The Dimabrush system has changed how we do store maintenance. It requires more focus on a detailed floor restoration process that needs extra time and attention. This means we have to adapt our operations to meet the new corporate standards. Because of this, I feel it is now important to have a Maintenance Academy Trainer.

 

1.2 - Maintenance Tasks

 

Our current maintenance tasks involve a range of responsibilities. This includes sweeping, scrubbing, managing garbage and recycling, maintaining spill stations, the creation of cardboard and plastic bails, and ensuring the overall cleanliness of the sales floor. Our current tasks require attention to detail and efficiency to meet the store's standards.

 

1.3 - Dimabrush System Impact

 

The Dimabrush system has changed how we maintain the store, putting more demand on the Maintenance team for new floor restoration efforts. To adapt efficiently, we need a dedicated Maintenance Academy Trainer. Retraining employees for their current tasks is vital, ensuring they have time for sales floor maintenance. Our current Academy Trainer handles cashiers and customer service, but adding more time for managing maintenance employees, which they lack, could impact their effectiveness towards customers in the store, affecting our CFF scores.

 

1.4 - Training Needs and Challenges

 

•  When to use 400 Grit restoration discs

•  When to use 1000 Grit restoration discs

•  When to use 1000 Grit polymer discs

•  When and how to change disc blades

•  Proper use for the Minuteman (Gary) and T500 (Christine) scrubbers

 

To adjust to the new system, we need to retrain our maintenance team. It's crucial to ensure they can handle their tasks efficiently and utilize the extra time for sales floor maintenance. However, our current Academy Trainer, managing cashiers and customer service, might find it tough to cover all the details of maintenance tasks. That's why we need a dedicated Maintenance Academy Trainer, to specialize in the unique training requirements of our maintenance team, making them more effective and focused.

After which, maintaining an appropriate inventory of polymer discs will be essential for daily use with the Minuteman and T500 scrubbers ensuring a consistent and polished appearance, aligning with the standards set by the new Dimabrush system.

 

1.5 - Market Support and Rationale

 

The implementation of the Dimabrush system is a Market initiative. In support of this transition, it stands to reason there will be additional resources and budget allocations earmarked to assist store managers in meeting the updated standards. This includes additional employees, in our case a new cart associate and Maintenance Academy Trainer, to meet the increased demands on store maintenance.
